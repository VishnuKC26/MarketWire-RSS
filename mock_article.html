Dow hits record high; Broadcom-led chip selloff weighs on Nasdaq

NEW YORK, June 4 (Reuters) - The Dow Jones Industrial Average surged to a fresh record high on Thursday, propelled by a strong rally in industrial and bank shares following positive macroeconomic data, while a steep selloff in chip stocks led by Broadcom dragged down the Nasdaq.

Stronger-than-expected payroll reports and declining unemployment numbers bolstered investor confidence in a soft-landing scenario for the U.S. economy, fueling broad gains across cyclical sectors.

"The market is displaying a classic rotation today. Value and cyclicals are taking the lead as investors gain confidence in the economic expansion, even as tech takes a breather," said Sarah Miller, chief market strategist at GreatHill Capital.

In contrast, technology shares faced head-winds. Broadcom fell 6.2% after warning of temporary supply chain issues in its high-end AI components, pulling down peer chipmakers including Nvidia and Advanced Micro Devices.

The S&P 500 remained flat, balancing out the tech selloff with financial and energy gains.
